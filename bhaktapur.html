Bhaktapur, located in the Kathmandu Valley of Nepal, is known as the "City of Devotees". Famous for its rich culture, exquisite temples, traditional arts, and pottery, Bhaktapur preserves centuries-old architecture and heritage. It is also famous for its festivals, traditional Newari cuisine, and vibrant local markets.

The city was a medieval kingdom and is home to landmarks such as the 55-Window Palace, Patan Durbar Square, and the Nyatapola Temple, one of the tallest pagoda-style temples in Nepal.
